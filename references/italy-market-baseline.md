## 3. Electricity Market Parameters (2024 data)

- **Total electricity demand:** ~312.7 TWh (Terna provisional)
- **Net imports:** ~51 TWh (~16.3% of demand) — one of the highest import-dependency rates in Europe
- **Generation mix (approx.):** Gas ~40%, renewables ~44% (hydro ~14%, solar ~13%, wind ~7%, other ~10%), nuclear imports ~16% (indirect, via net imports)
- **Renewable capacity target 2030:** 131 GW (PNIEC 2024), including 79 GW solar, 28 GW wind
- **Renewables in electricity (2023):** ~38.1% of gross consumption; target 63% by 2030
- **Incentivised electricity:** ~53.5 TWh (2024); system cost ~€8.9bn (+26% vs 2023)
- **Day-ahead price:** Italy (PUN) trades at a persistent premium to Germany (DE) and France (FR); ARERA launched market inquiry October 2024 into potential capacity withholding
- **15-minute intraday intervals:** EU moved to 15-minute trading intervals in day-ahead market from 30 September 2025 (Electricity Regulation EU/2019/943)
- **Capacity Market:** Operational since 2022; strike price mechanism; payments for existing capacity rose from €33k/MW (2022–24) to €45k/MW (2025)

---

## 4. Gas Market Parameters (2024 data)

- **Domestic gas production:** ~2.9 bcm (~<5% of consumption); declining trend; Argo Cassiopea (Eni) now producing
- **Gas imports:** ~90% of consumption; pipeline (Algeria via Transmed/Greenstream, Azerbaijan via TAP) + LNG
- **LNG share:** ~25% of gas imports (2024); growing given post-Ukraine diversification
- **Italian hub:** PSV (Punto di Scambio Virtuale) trades at a premium to TTF; government exploring structural measures to reduce spread
- **Gas in power mix:** Gas remains ~40% of electricity generation; direct linkage between PSV and Italian wholesale power prices
- **Vulnerability protection service:** Replaces old protected market from January 2024; ARERA updates conditions quarterly; applies to vulnerable domestic customers only

## 6. Italy-Specific Structural Tensions Relevant to Regulatory Analysis

1. **Import dependency vs. decarbonisation:** Decarbonising Italy's power mix requires massive renewable build-out and storage investment, but the country remains structurally dependent on cross-border imports for grid balancing. Any regulation affecting interconnectors or cross-border capacity is disproportionately significant for Italy.

2. **PSV-TTF spread:** Italy's gas prices consistently exceed European benchmarks, feeding through directly to electricity wholesale prices. Regulations touching gas network tariffs, gas storage, or LNG terminal access have material secondary effects on electricity market functioning.

3. **e-distribuzione dominance and tariff regulation:** With 85.1% of distribution under a single Enel entity, any EU regulation changing DSO tariff methodology or investment recovery frameworks will have concentrated impact on e-distribuzione's regulatory asset base and revenue.

4. **Incomplete retail liberalisation:** The transition to full retail market liberalisation (completed for non-vulnerable customers in 2024) means that EU consumer protection provisions interact with an Italian market still adjusting to post-protection dynamics. Enel Energia's dominant retail position attracts regulatory scrutiny.

5. **Renewables incentive cost escalation:** The cost of renewable incentives reached €8.9bn in 2024 (+26%), borne through system charges. EU regulations accelerating renewable deployment will further increase this cost, affecting tariff structures and Enel's distribution revenues.

6. **Nuclear re-entry ambiguity:** Italy's nuclear re-entry via Nuclitalia is at an early legislative stage. Any EU regulation touching nuclear (Euratom rules, taxonomy, state aid for nuclear), or AI/data centre energy demand projections, is directly relevant to Nuclitalia's investment planning.

7. **Data centre electricity demand:** Italy hosts ~12% of Europe's data centres, concentrated in the northwest; demand is growing at double-digit rates and constituted ~3% of national electricity demand in 2024. Regulations affecting data centre energy consumption or grid connection create asymmetric demand impacts on Italy's grid.
